Moisturizers are a necessary staple of every woman's skincare routine. Whether you call it face cream, day cream, or cold cream, its job is simple -- to hydrate and bring moisture to your face. Indeed, in your younger days, that's all you needed it to do.

But for any woman over 40, it's no longer enough for soft, youthful, healthy-looking skin. An ideal moisturizer should also address the unique needs of mature skin with anti-aging and restorative ingredients, including:

Key Ingredients In A High-Quality Moisturizer

STEM CELLS

These powerful plant stem cells have been scientifically proven to promote skin cell regeneration. They correct existing signs of aging while delaying further damage as well.

GLYCERIN

This "super moisturizer" attracts up to 1000x its weight in water! Which means maximum hydration and tighter, firmer, more healthy looking skin.

HYALURONIC ACID

After the age of 20, we lose 1% of collagen per year. Hyaluronic acid helps promote collagen and elastic production to plump and firm your skin while reducing wrinkles and fine lines.

VITAMIN E

Vitamin E is a potent antioxidant which assists in various kinds of cellular restoration, protection, and healing. It’s used to both restore and repair a range of skin conditions.

SHEA BUTTER

High in fatty acids, shea butter has both soothing and anti-aging properties. It makes rough, textured, mature skin feel smooth and silky soft while also reducing signs of aging.

Ingredients To Avoid

Chemical Additives

Artificial chemicals and toxins can limit your skin's ability to naturally breathe, exfoliate, and even heal. Some can even worsen existing skin conditions, inflame or irritate, or cause acne or eczema.

Parabens

Parabens can cause allergic reactions, reduce collagen production, and even disrupt hormones and fertility organs. We always recommend “paraben-free” products whenever possible.

Overwhelmingly Strong Fragrances

Strongly scented products create a strong, nauseating reaction and can cause immediate irritation and inflammation, especially for those who have sensitive skin.

What Else To Consider When Buying A Moisturizer

Active ingredients to correct AND delay the signs of aging

Most moisturizers do nothing more than hydrate the skin, and protect against some future signs of aging. Studies suggest that hydration helps maintain the skin’s barrier and promote regular melanin production, so it’s important, but it’s not everything. A good moisturizer will contain active ingredients that actually work together to correct signs of aging like fine lines, wrinkles and sagging skin (and not just delay them).

Money Back Guarantee

Americans spend millions of dollars every year for youthful skin, and they still find themselves unhappy with an apparent lack of results. It’s better—and safer—to invest in a product that offers a 100% money-back, no-questions-asked guarantee to show the company's faith in the potency of its product.
